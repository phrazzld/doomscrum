Verdict — A, corner stamp (implemented)

Why A wins: it is the only option that scores on all four requirements at once — names the action AND the consequence in readable type from the first centimeter of drag (F and B name it too late or too small), makes the threshold crossing unmistakable via the outline→fill state change (the fill is commitment feedback, the one place the aesthetic law permits it), keeps the sacred caption zone untouched (D floods it, C collides with the chip row), and borrows a gesture grammar (Tinder stamps) every shortform user already knows — zero teaching cost for a product whose hook is "feels like a native shortform app." E remains the runner-up as a quiet composable layer if the stamps ever feel loud in daily triage.
